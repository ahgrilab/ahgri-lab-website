Principal Investigator

Floyid Nicolas, Ph.D.

Assistant Professor of Agricultural Water Management

Dr. Nicolas is an Assistant Professor of Agrohydrology and Water Management in the Department of Biological and Ecological Engineering at Oregon State University. He is based at the Central Oregon Agricultural Research and Extension Center (COAREC) in Madras, Oregon. His research focuses on agricultural water management and irrigation efficiency, soil hydrology and groundwater sustainability, evapotranspiration measurement and modeling, and climate-smart agriculture.

His interdisciplinary approach aims to enhance irrigation efficiency, evapotranspiration estimates, crop production, groundwater sustainability, and quality through the development of comprehensive modeling frameworks and decision-support tools to support farmers and policymakers in making data-driven management decisions.
